William Wymark Jacobs

1863 – 1943

"Be careful what you wish for, you just might get it."

About the Author

William Wymark Jacobs was an eminent English author best remembered for his masterful horror story "The Monkey's Paw".

While his modern legacy heavily rests on his macabre supernatural tales, the vast majority of Jacobs's actual literary output was lighthearted and humorous.

He was a highly celebrated humorist during his era, drawing praise from contemporary literary legends like Henry James, G.K. Chesterton, and H.G. Wells. He managed to perfectly transition daily civilian life into captivating fiction.

Early Life & Childhood

Born on September 8, 1863, in the Wapping district of East London, Jacobs grew up tightly connected to maritime life.

His father was a wharf manager at the South Devon Wharf on the River Thames. Spending his childhood around the vibrant, chaotic docklands heavily inspired his creative mind.

The seafaring men, sailors, dockworkers, and transient travelers he encountered daily became the vivid templates for the colorful characters found throughout his short story collections later in life.

Famous Works & Books

W.W. Jacobs published thirteen short story collections and five novels. Here are a few of his most significant works:

1902 · Short Story Collection

The Lady of the Barge

This critical collection contains his absolute masterpiece, "The Monkey's Paw", a terrifying story exploring three cursed wishes and the deadly consequences of tampering with fate.

1896 · Debut Anthology

Many Cargoes

Jacobs's debut anthology introduced the world to his vibrant seafaring characters. Set aboard merchant ships and in British ports, the stories blend sharp wit with maritime adventure.

1914 · Short Story Collection

Night Watches

One of his finest later collections, blending comedic tales of dock life and night-shift workers with darker undercurrents and ghostly, unsettling encounters.

1902 · Most Famous Story

The Monkey's Paw

Arguably the most famous short horror story in the English language. The tale of the White family and a cursed talisman that grants three wishes with devastating consequences remains chilling over a century later.
